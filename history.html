HISTORY OF PARFUMES

Throughout history, fragrance has been more than just a scent,
it has symbolized power and prestige.
Its timeless elegance continues to captivate, making it a lasting mark of sophistication.

HISTORY

You don’t need to be a history expert to be enchanted by the captivating legacy of fragrance. For millennia, perfume has served as a divine offering, a symbol of wealth, a tool of seduction, and a source of pleasure.
Though its origins are lost to time, its story stretches from Ancient Egypt to Greece and Rome, where rosewater flowed in fountains, and to the opulent court of Louis XIV, who required a new scent every day.Modern perfumery emerged in the late 19th century with the invention of synthetic ingredients, giving rise to legendary houses like Coty, Guerlain, and Roger & Gallet.
Coco Chanel’s No. 5 revolutionized perfume, establishing it as a luxury staple, while Estée Lauder’s Youth Dew in the 1950s made fragrance a daily indulgence rather than a rare treat.This fascinating history, spanning centuries and cultures, is an invitation to discover the enduring allure of perfume—an art form that continues to captivate and transform.

Francis Kurkdjian, born in Paris on May 14, 1969, is a renowned Armenian perfumer and entrepreneur, acclaimed for his exceptional contributions to the fragrance industry. At just 26 years old, he gained international fame for creating Le Male for Jean Paul Gaultier, one of the best-selling perfumes of all time. Over the course of his career, Kurkdjian has crafted over 40 iconic fragrances for leading global brands, including Elie Saab Le Parfum (Elie Saab), My Burberry (Burberry), L'Extase (Nina Ricci), Narciso Rodriguez for Her (Narciso Rodriguez), Iris Nobile (Aqua di Parma), and Eau Noir (Dior). In 2001, he was awarded the prestigious Prix François Coty for his lifetime achievements in perfumery.

Bertrand Duchaufour is a distinguished French perfumer known for his exceptional talent in creating unforgettable fragrances. His name is familiar to many, as he has crafted some of the most celebrated scents, particularly for the renowned niche brand, l’Artisan Parfumeur, where he served as their in-house perfumer. Among his standout creations are By Night White and By Night Black for Profumi del Forte, two exquisite examples of his artistry. Duchaufour honed his craft at Florasynth before expanding his expertise while working with Symrise, shaping his career as one of the industry's most respected figures.

Luca Maffei is a talented young Italian perfumer and founder of his own company, Atelier Fragranze Milano. Since joining the Société Française des Parfumeurs (SFP) in 2014, he has crafted over 50 fragrances for both mainstream and niche brands, as well as air scents and candles. Fascinated by the world of raw materials and the mysterious alchemy of blending them, Maffei has made a mark with creations like Black Pepper & Sandalwood for Acca Kappa and Néa for JUL ET MAD Paris, both of which won the prestigious "Art and Olfaction Awards" in 2015 and 2016. Additionally, his work on 1..1 for Nobile 1942, featuring papyrus flower at its heart, is another standout achievement in his growing portfolio.

Rodrigo Flores-Roux was born in Mexico and moved to France in 1989, where he attended the prestigious ISIPCA school of perfumery in Versailles. After completing an internship with the renowned French perfumer Jean-Claude Ellena at Givaudan, he began his career by winning briefs, starting with shampoos before creating his first fine fragrance for Clinique. Over the years, he has crafted iconic scents for major brands like Calvin Klein, Elizabeth Arden, and Santi Burgas (Palindromes). Since joining Givaudan in 2007, he has continued to shape the fragrance industry as a Senior Perfumer.
